DOS AND DON'TS TRAVELLING IN AFRICA

DOs

  • PASSPORTS PASSPORTS PASSPORTS. Make sure your passport is valid for 6 months after the date you intend to leave the country. You will need to have at least 2 blank pages for every country you intend to travel to.
  • VISAS. Click here for details
  • CLOTHES. A lot of people don't realise how cold it gets in the evenings. You will most likely spend a lot of time outdoors so take some warm clothes as well. One word. LAYERS.
  • SUNCREAM. Typically a difficult one to find when you're there, so take some with you.
  • TORCH. Power cuts are quite frequent.
  • LIP BALM. An essential to protect against the dry African air, the wind in your face and the insane amount of dust during dry season.
    •

DON'Ts

  • BE A CREATURE OF HABIT. Try as many local cuisines as you can.
  • BE CARELESS WITH YOUR BELONGINGS. Awareness please.
  • DRIVING. Don't drive around with your doors unlocked and your windows down
  • WANDERING AROUND. Fine to do, just not in unknown areas. Stick to areas you know are safe
  • CUT YOUR TRIP TOO SHORT. You could spend months exploring and barely scratch the surface
